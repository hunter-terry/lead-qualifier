How it actually works — no code required

What happens when a lead comes in

This walks through lead-qualifier, a real automation that scores incoming sales leads with AI and sorts them — hot leads for immediate follow-up, cold or broken ones set aside. Both examples below are real runs, executed live, not mocked up.

The pipeline

Four steps, the same shape whether it's built in n8n, Zapier, or a plain script.

1
Lead arrives
A new inquiry lands — from a web form, a CRM export, however it currently reaches you.
2
AI reads it
A local model reads the message and rates intent, urgency, and a 1–10 fit score.
3
Checked, not trusted
A plain rule check confirms the AI's answer is actually usable — before anything moves forward.
4
Sorted automatically
Hot leads go one way for immediate follow-up. Cold or broken ones go another. Nothing sits unsorted.

This is the actual point of the exercise. The lead on the right had no name and no message — and the AI still confidently invented an intent, an urgency, and a score, same as it did for a real lead. Left alone, that's a blank contact silently entering your pipeline as if it were real. The rule-based check on step 3 is what caught it and stopped it — not the AI noticing its own mistake, because it didn't.
